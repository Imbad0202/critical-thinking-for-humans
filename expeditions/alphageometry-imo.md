# Expedition Pack: AlphaGeometry on Olympiad Geometry

`pack_id: alphageometry-imo`

A neuro-symbolic system solved 25 of 30 olympiad geometry problems with
HUMAN-READABLE proofs — a language model proposes auxiliary constructions, a
symbolic engine proves. The audit contrast: 'human-readable + symbolically
checked' is a different verification standard from AlphaProof's machine-formal
Lean proofs.

## problem

**Statement.** Can a machine produce *human-readable, checkable* proofs for olympiad-level Euclidean plane geometry — the kind of problem (incidence, concurrence, angle/length equalities, cyclic quadrilaterals) posed at the International Mathematical Olympiad — without being trained on human proof demonstrations, which barely exist in machine-usable form for geometry?

**Answer.** Yes, for most such problems. AlphaGeometry (DeepMind) solved **25 of 30** problems on the IMO-AG-30 benchmark (latest olympiad-level geometry problems), versus **10** for the previous best automated method (Wu's method), approaching the average score of an IMO gold medallist. Crucially the proofs are emitted in classical synthetic-geometry steps (angle chasing, similar triangles, ratios) that a human coach can read and check, and every solution was verified by the symbolic engine; a selection was also reviewed by a human olympiad gold-medallist coach.

**Accessibility note.** The *problems* are accessible to any strong high-schooler — they need a clever auxiliary construction (drop a perpendicular, add a midpoint, draw a circle) and then a chain of deductions. What is *not* human-reproducible is the search: the auxiliary-construction space is unbounded, and AlphaGeometry's value is that it learned to propose the right constructs from synthetic data alone. An auditor can read each individual proof step but could not have generated the construction-finding search that produced them. That gap — readable output, superhuman generator — is exactly what makes this a good auditing exercise.

## history

**How long open / who.** Automated theorem proving for geometry is old: Wu Wen-Tsün's algebraic method (1970s) and Chou/Gao/Zhang's deductive-database and area methods could decide large classes of geometry statements. The open difficulty was not decidability in principle but (a) producing *olympiad-hard* proofs under time limits and (b) producing *human-readable* synthetic proofs rather than opaque polynomial-coordinate certificates. The data-scarcity problem — almost no machine-verifiable corpus of human geometry proofs to learn from — blocked the machine-learning approaches that were transforming other domains. AlphaGeometry (Trinh, Wu, Le, He, Luong; Nature, 17 Jan 2024) attacked exactly that data gap.

**Dead end 1 — coordinate/algebraic brute force (Wu's method, Gröbner bases).** Reduce the problem to polynomial systems and decide by algebra. This *works* as a decision procedure and is complete on its fragment, but on IMO-AG-30 the previous best automated method solved only 10, and the output is a polynomial certificate, not a readable geometric argument. Dead end for the *human-readable proof* goal even where it succeeds on the *true/false* goal.

**Dead end 2 — learn proofs from a human corpus.** The standard ML recipe (imitate human-written proofs) is a dead end for geometry: human geometry proofs almost never exist in machine-verifiable form, so there is no corpus to imitate. This data scarcity is the specific obstacle the paper names.

**Dead end 3 — pure symbolic deductive closure (deductive database alone).** Exhaustively chase all derivable facts from the premises. This terminates and is readable, but without new points it cannot solve the problems whose solution *requires* an auxiliary construction — and the construction space is infinite, so blind enumeration is a dead end.

**Dead end 4 — pure LLM end-to-end proof generation.** Ask a language model to write the whole proof. This is a dead end on rigor: an LLM left to free-form will produce fluent but unsound steps. AlphaGeometry's design confines the LLM to *proposing constructions only*, leaving every deduction to the symbolic engine.

## step_graph

- **S0 — Frame the real obstacle as data, not decidability.** `shape_question` The question is not "is olympiad geometry decidable" (Wu's method already answers a version of that) but "can we get *human-readable* olympiad proofs *without a human proof corpus to learn from*." Reframing fixes the target on the data-scarcity bottleneck. Check: the abstract names "the high cost of translating human proofs into machine-verifiable format" and "severe scarcity of training data" as the core obstacle.
- **S1 — Split the system into propose vs. prove.** `lemma_decomposition` Decompose proving into two organs: a *symbolic deduction engine* that derives only sound consequences, and a *language model* that proposes auxiliary constructions to break deduction deadlocks. Soundness lives entirely in the symbolic half; creativity lives entirely in the neural half. Check: abstract — neural LM "guides a symbolic deduction engine through infinite branching points."
- **S2 — Change the representation of training data: synthesize, don't collect.** `representation_shift` Instead of representing the learning problem as "imitate human proofs," represent it as "generate millions of (premise, construction, proof) triples by random construction + symbolic deduction, then learn the construction step." This sidesteps the missing corpus entirely. Check: abstract — "synthesizing millions of theorems and proofs across different levels of complexity," "trained from scratch on … large-scale synthetic data."
- **S3 — Constrain the LM to constructions only (kill unsound free-form proving).** `kill_criteria` Refuse the design where the LM writes whole proofs; it may only emit auxiliary points/objects, after which the symbolic engine must close the proof. Any step the symbolic engine cannot certify is rejected. Check: DeepMind write-up — LM "predicts which new constructs would be most useful to add," symbolic engine "make[s] further deductions"; every solution "checked and verified by computer."
- **S4 — Benchmark against the human gold-medallist bar.** `milestone_rewrite` Rewrite "solve geometry" into a measurable milestone: score on 30 latest olympiad problems under competition time limits, compared to the average IMO gold medallist. Check: abstract — "AlphaGeometry solves 25 … approaching the performance of an average IMO gold medallist"; prior best = 10.
- **S5 — Validate readability on concrete solved cases by a human expert.** `small_case_probe` Probe individual solved problems and hand them to a human olympiad coach to confirm the steps are classical and checkable, not just machine-true. Check: DeepMind write-up — coach Evan Chen evaluated a selection and called the output "verifiable and clean," using "angles and similar triangles just as students do."

## breakthrough

**S2 (synthetic-data representation shift)** is the breakthrough. The community was stuck because everyone read the problem as "learn from human proofs," and geometry has almost no human proofs in machine-verifiable form — so the ML toolkit looked inapplicable. AlphaGeometry's move was to stop trying to collect data and instead *generate* it: run random constructions through the symbolic engine, observe which auxiliary objects were needed, and treat that as supervised signal for the construction-proposing LM. It eluded the community because it inverts the usual dependency (you normally need a prover-checkable corpus before you can train; here the prover *manufactures* the corpus), and because the payoff only appears once it is paired with S1's clean propose/prove split that keeps soundness with the symbolic engine. S3 (confining the LM to constructions) is what makes the readable, checkable output possible — and is the exact axis that distinguishes this pack from the Lean-formal AlphaProof line.

## audit_targets

- **T1 — "Human-readable + symbolically checked" is a *different* verification standard from "machine-formal (Lean)."** *Objection:* an auditor might assume any AI math result is trustworthy only if Lean-checked, and dismiss AlphaGeometry as "not formally verified." *Resolution:* AlphaGeometry's checker is its own symbolic deduction engine plus human expert review of readable synthetic steps — a *sound by construction* engine emitting classical-geometry proofs, not a Lean kernel. Both are real verification; they certify different objects (a readable geometric argument vs. a kernel-checked formal term). Confirmed first-party: every solution "checked and verified by computer," plus Evan Chen's review. The audit lesson: name *which* checker certified *which* artifact before trusting or rejecting.
- **T2 — The 25/30 score is first-party; the 25.9 gold-medallist average is NOT in the text I read.** *Objection:* "approaching the performance of an average IMO gold medallist" is qualitative in the abstract; the crisp "25.9" is easy to over-cite as if it were a headline paper number. *Resolution:* I confirmed 25/30 and the qualitative gold-medallist comparison from the abstract; the 25.9 figure comes from DeepMind's blog/secondary coverage, not the paper body I could access. Treat 25.9 as provenance-blog, not provenance-paper. See unverified_claims.
- **T3 — The named engine "DDAR/DD+AR" is not confirmed from the paper body.** *Objection:* secondary sources call the symbolic engine "DDAR" (deductive database + algebraic/arithmetic reasoning); a careless auditor will attribute that acronym to the abstract. *Resolution:* the abstract I read says only "symbolic deduction engine." The DDAR / DD+AR naming is from blog and follow-up papers. The *mechanism* (symbolic deduction engine guided by the LM) is first-party; the *acronym* is secondary.
- **T4 — "Solves 25" ≠ "geometry is solved."** *Objection:* approaching gold-medallist average reads as superhuman/general. *Resolution:* scope is *Euclidean plane geometry* on a 30-problem benchmark of one problem type; 5 of 30 were unsolved, and the result does not extend to combinatorics, number theory, or algebra olympiad problems. The milestone (S4) is narrow by design.
- **T5 — "Without human demonstrations" ≠ "without human knowledge."** *Objection:* the title suggests the system bootstrapped from nothing. *Resolution:* it sidesteps human *proof* demonstrations via synthetic data, but the symbolic engine encodes human-curated geometry deduction rules and the construction vocabulary; "no demonstrations" is a claim about the training corpus, not about zero human priors.
